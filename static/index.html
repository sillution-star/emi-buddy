Learn
Why flat rate can be misleading.
💵
What is flat rate?
Interest is calculated on the original loan amount for the entire tenure. Even though you're paying back every month, the interest doesn't reduce.
📈
What is reducing balance?
Interest is calculated on the remaining balance each month. As you pay EMIs, the balance drops, and so does your interest. This reflects the actual cost.
💡
Why the difference matters
A "10% flat" and "10% reducing" are very different loans. The flat rate loan costs significantly more. Always compare loans on reducing balance rate.
✅
How to protect yourself
Ask for the reducing balance rate or the APR (Annual Percentage Rate). Compare loans only on this number. Use EMI Buddy to verify.
